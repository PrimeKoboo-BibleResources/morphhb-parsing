Login

Logging in to the site is required, to facilitate tracking contributions by members. Your username can be the same as your email address. We do not yet have a facility to help you with forgotten passwords, so please be careful to remember them.

Registration

Registration requires a valid email address. This will be used for confirmation of your registration to the site. Your choice of Username must be unique, for login purposes. The password requirements are listed on the form, and your password confirmation must match the original.

To maintain the integrity of our site, we are unfortunately required to use the Enter What You See mechanism. If you are unable to decipher it, there is a Change Image option underneath.

Chapter Selection

Once you are logged in, you will need to select a chapter to parse. Any change to the Chapter dropdown will select that chapter. Otherwise, you can use the Select button to choose the current one.

Text Appearance

The currently selected word is highlighted in green. When a word has a parsing value, it will have a brown background. This will make it more clear what needs to be done. Hovering over a word will show you its augmented Strong number, and if it has a parsing, that will appear too.

Parsing

Morph Codes Language Segments Morph Hint Navigation Errors

Morph Codes

We have a web page describing the Hebrew Morphology Codes we are using for parsing.

Language

The language, Hebrew or Aramaic, for a given word is already filled in when the word is selected. If you are working in a passage of scripture where the language is incorrect, you can change the Language choice beneath the word box, and future selections will use that language. To change the current word, simply backspace over the language code, and type or click the correct one.

Segments

In the word box, the selected word is segmented to distinguish between prefixes, the main word and suffixes. Each segment is highlighted in green as you parse it.

Morph Hint

The hint dropdown, beneath the word box, is meant to be an aid to parsing. The letter or key at the left can be entered from the keyboard, or the line in the dropdown can be clicked, to choose it.

If necessary, you can use an ‘x’ in a parsing field, if there are other values to follow in that segment. To skip to the next segment, enter a slash (/). If you are in the final segment, you can apply the parsing.

Navigation

You can apply a parsing to the selected word by hitting Enter or clicking Apply, at the end of the parsing.

When you apply a parsing, you will be automatically advanced to the next word. You can also click any word in the chapter, to select it for parsing. You can also use the next (<) and previous (>) word buttons beside the word box.

Errors

If you should enter an incorrect parsing value, the error will be indicated by a change of color in the Morphology box, and a red X at the right. A description of the error will appear in the Explanation box. You can simply backspace in the Morphology box to remove the error, and type a correct value, as indicated in the hint dropdown.

If you find that an existing parsing is incorrect, you can make the appropriate corrections, and apply the parsing normally. Your correction will be recorded, and an editor will be able to resolve the issue.

Explanation

The Explanation box will give a verbal interpretation of the parsing code, as you enter it. This will allow you to understand the full meaning of your parsing, or to identify any errors you might make.
